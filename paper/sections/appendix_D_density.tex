\subsection{Alternative Fixed Effects: Ward-Pair}

Table~\ref{tab:fe_pair_year} replaces segment fixed effects with ward-pair fixed effects, which pool across all segments of a given ward pair. This is a coarser comparison---ward-pair borders can stretch for miles through different neighborhoods---but it uses more variation. The estimates remain negative, but they are attenuated relative to the main segment FE estimates in Table~\ref{tab:segment_fe_500}.

\subsection{Donut RD Robustness}

One concern with spatial RD designs is that observations precisely at the boundary may be unusual---for example, if ward boundaries follow major streets that independently affect development. Figure~\ref{fig:rd_donut} implements a donut specification \citep{barreca_saving_2011, barreca_heapinginduced_2016} that excludes parcels within 25 or 50 feet of the boundary. In both donut plots, the fitted discontinuity remains negative, suggesting the main pattern is not driven only by parcels right at the cutoff.
