\subsection{Heterogeneity by Strictness Gap}

The main border discontinuity is stronger for ward pairs with larger differences in aldermanic stringency. Figure~\ref{fig:rd_gap_split} splits the sample at the median ward-pair strictness gap, measured as the median absolute difference in own-side and neighboring-side aldermanic stringency within each ward pair. The above-median-gap sample shows a clear negative discontinuity in both FAR and DUPAC, while the below-median-gap sample is flatter.

\subsection{Donut RD Robustness}

One concern with spatial RD designs is that observations precisely at the boundary may be unusual---for example, if ward boundaries follow major streets that independently affect development. Figure~\ref{fig:rd_donut} implements a donut specification \citep{barreca_saving_2011, barreca_heaping-induced_2016} matched to the main 250-foot all-construction specification by excluding parcels within 25 or 50 feet of the boundary. In all four panels, the fitted discontinuity remains negative, which suggests that the main pattern is not driven only by parcels right at the cutoff.
