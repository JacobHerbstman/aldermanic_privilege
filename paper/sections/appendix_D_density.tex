This is a coarser comparison---ward-pair borders can stretch for miles through very different neighborhoods---but it uses more variation. \subsection{Heterogeneity by Strictness Gap}

The main border discontinuity is stronger for ward pairs with larger differences in aldermanic stringency. Figure~\ref{fig:rd_gap_split} splits the sample at the median ward-pair strictness gap, measured as the median absolute difference in own-side and neighboring-side aldermanic stringency within each ward pair. The above-median-gap sample shows a clear negative discontinuity in both FAR and DUPAC, while the below-median-gap sample is much flatter.

\subsection{Donut RD Robustness}

One concern with spatial RD designs is that observations precisely at the boundary may be unusual---for example, if ward boundaries follow major streets that independently affect development. Figure~\ref{fig:rd_donut} implements a donut specification \citep{barreca_saving_2011, barreca_heaping-induced_2016} that excludes parcels within 25 or 50 feet of the boundary. In both donut plots, the fitted discontinuity remains negative, which suggests that the main pattern is not driven only by parcels right at the cutoff.

\subsection{All Construction}

The main analysis restricts to multifamily buildings with 2 or more units. Figure~\ref{fig:rd_all_units} includes all new construction, including single-family homes. The plotted discontinuity appears smaller than in the multifamily-only sample, as expected given that single-family construction has less variation in density and is less exposed to aldermanic discretion.
