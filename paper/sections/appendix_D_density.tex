\subsection{Paired Balance in the Density Samples}

Tables~\ref{tab:density_paired_balance} and~\ref{tab:density_paired_balance_multifamily} compare parcels on the less and more stringent sides of the same boundary segments in the 500ft all-construction and multifamily density samples.
Each row first averages parcel characteristics within a segment side, then reports the paired difference between the more and less stringent sides.
The two sides look similar on planned-development status and zoned FAR.
A few amenity distances are statistically different: in the all-construction sample, parcels on the more stringent side are about 18 meters closer to parks; in the multifamily sample, parcels on the more stringent side are about 44 meters closer to the CBD.
These differences are small relative to the cross-segment variation in amenities, with normalized differences no larger than 0.17 standard deviations.

\subsection{Cross-Border Stringency Gap}

Figures~\ref{fig:rd_gap_split_above} and~\ref{fig:rd_gap_split_below} split the density RD display by the median ward-pair stringency gap, with all-construction and multifamily samples shown side by side.
The above-median-gap sample shows a sharper negative break in both FAR and DUPAC; the below-median-gap sample is flatter.
I treat this as a supporting diagnostic because it asks whether the visual discontinuity is largest where the identifying contrast is stronger.
