\subsection{Permit Summary Statistics}
\label{app:permit_summary_stats}

Table~\ref{tab:permit_summary_stats} compares the high- and low-discretion permit groups used throughout the paper. High-discretion permits are much slower on average and display substantially more cross-alderman heterogeneity in mean processing times, consistent with the idea that these permits are most exposed to discretionary review.

\subsection{Permit Volume and Processing Time}

Table~\ref{tab:permit_volume_correlation} reports the correlation between individual permit processing time and ward-month permit volume. The negative correlation for high-discretion permits is consistent with political and regulatory friction reducing throughput where discretionary review is most important, while the positive correlation for low-discretion permits suggests routine administrative scale rather than aldermanic discretion.
