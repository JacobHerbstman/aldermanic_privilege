\section{Density at Ward Boundaries}
\label{sec:empirical_results_density}

\subsection{Border Fixed Effects Regressions}

Table~\ref{tab:density_main_table} reports the main density estimates.
To compare truly similar neighborhoods, I build border segments along each ward boundary that are approximately a quarter-mile in length. 
Since some ward borders are several miles long, this allows me to focus on truly local comparisons rather than comparing parcels in different community areas with different demand and demographic compositions.
Appendix Figure~\ref{fig:pruned_border_segments} maps the boundary segments that enter the analysis.

In the multifamily sample within 500ft of a ward boundary, FAR is about 24\% lower and DUPAC about 28\% lower on the more stringent side.
A continuous comparison based on the score difference between neighboring aldermen implies that, at a boundary where their scores differ by one standard deviation, multifamily FAR is about 20\% lower and DUPAC about 16\% lower on the more stringent side.

I estimate:
%
\begin{equation} \label{eq:segment_fe}
\begin{aligned}
    \ln(Y_{iwst}) ={}& \tau \text{MoreStringent}_{iwst} + \eta \overline{S}_{ww't}
    + \lambda_L D^-_{iwst} + \lambda_S D^+_{iwst} + X_i'\theta \\
    &+ \alpha_{\text{zone group}} + \alpha_{\text{segment}} + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{iwst}
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
%
where $Y_{iwst}$ is a density outcome for parcel $i$ in ward $w$ on boundary segment $s$, built in year $t$; $w'$ is the ward across the boundary; $\overline{S}_{ww't}=(S_{wt}+S_{w't})/2$ is the average score of the two aldermen; $D^-_{iwst}$ and $D^+_{iwst}$ are side-specific distances to the ward boundary on the lenient and stringent sides; and $X_i$ collects own-side demographic controls.
The fixed effects enter additively: zoning-group fixed effects absorb differences across broad zoning environments, segment fixed effects absorb differences across boundary segments, and year fixed effects absorb citywide trends.
The side-specific distance terms absorb smooth within-side gradients in development density as one moves toward or away from the boundary.
Zoning-group fixed effects ensure that parcels are compared within broad zoning environments rather than across fundamentally different parts of the zoning code, which could be 
unrelated to the alderman's behavior and simply a feature of the neighborhood.
I estimate the specification for all new construction and for multifamily new construction from 2006-2022 within 500ft of a ward boundary.
Standard errors are clustered conservatively at the ward-pair level.

Table~\ref{tab:density_main_table} reports both versions of the density comparison.
Panel A reports the binary comparison visualized in Figure~\ref{fig:rd_fe_plots}.
Panel B replaces the side indicator with $(S_{wt}-S_{w't})/2$, so crossing a boundary changes the treatment by the full score difference between the two aldermen.

\subsection{Visual Evidence}

Figure~\ref{fig:rd_fe_plots} plots the 500ft all-construction and multifamily samples using a residualized local-linear RD display with five binned points on each side of the boundary.
The plots provide a visual check that the discontinuity occurs at the ward boundary and has the same sign as the fixed-effects estimates.
These results are somewhat stronger than the regression form and suggest some developer sorting going on at ward boundaries, as there are visual drops in density on the most stringent side, particularly in the bins immediately adjacent to the boundary.

\subsection{Supporting Checks}

Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_density} reports the rest of the density evidence.
Appendix Figure~\ref{fig:pruned_border_segments} maps the boundary segments that enter the analysis.
Appendix Table~\ref{tab:fe_pruned} shows that the main estimates are robust to dropping ward-pair-era combinations flagged as confounded by major arterials or bodies of water.
Appendix Tables~\ref{tab:density_paired_balance} and~\ref{tab:density_paired_balance_multifamily} compare parcels on the less and more stringent sides of the same ward-boundary segments.
Some amenity distances are statistically different across sides, but the gaps are small in magnitude and modest relative to the variation across segments.
Appendix Figures~\ref{fig:rd_gap_split_above} and~\ref{fig:rd_gap_split_below} split the visual density evidence by the size of the ward-pair stringency gap.
Appendix Figures~\ref{fig:rd_placebo_shift_neg} and~\ref{fig:rd_placebo_shift_pos} shift the cutoff 500ft to either side of the true boundary and show no comparable discontinuity at those placebo cutoffs.
Appendix Figures~\ref{fig:rd_donut_25ft} and~\ref{fig:rd_donut_50ft} exclude parcels within 25ft or 50ft of the ward boundary, and Appendix Tables~\ref{tab:corner_clean_compare} and~\ref{tab:corner_clean_compare_multifamily} drop new developments that are plausibly close to a different border segment.
