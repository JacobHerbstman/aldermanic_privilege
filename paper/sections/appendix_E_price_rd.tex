\section{Additional Price Boundary Checks}
\label{sec:appendix_price_rd}

This appendix reports supporting checks for the rent and home-sales boundary comparisons in Section~\ref{sec:empirical_results_prices}.
Rent and sale-price outcomes are deflated to 2022 dollars using the monthly Chicago CPI-U all-items series.
The price specifications use external amenity controls: distance to the nearest school, Chicago Park District park-boundary polygon, major street, and Lake Michigan.
The rental specifications also control for distance to the nearest CTA stop open by the listing month.

\subsection{Paired Balance in the Rental Sample}

Table~\ref{tab:rental_rd_paired_amenity_balance} compares listed-rent observations on the less and more stringent sides of the same ward-boundary segments.
The table averages each amenity distance within a segment side and reports the paired more-stringent-minus-less-stringent difference.
Park and major-road distances are marginally different across sides, but the gaps are about 20 feet and 16 feet, respectively, with normalized differences below 0.06 standard deviations.

\subsection{Placebo Cutoffs}

Figures~\ref{fig:rent_placebo_boundaries} and~\ref{fig:sales_placebo_boundaries} repeat the main rent and sales boundary plots after moving the cutoff 1000ft inside each side of the true ward boundary.
Each shifted cutoff uses a 500ft window around the fake cutoff, so the placebo windows do not overlap the main true-boundary sample.
These fake cutoffs ask whether the estimated price difference appears only where aldermanic authority changes, rather than at arbitrary points nearby.
