# Generated-score uncertainty

This audit measures how uncertainty in the estimated alderman stringency score propagates into the paper's main estimates. It does not alter production scores, analysis data, figures, tables, or manuscript text.

## Bootstrap

The bootstrap is run separately for the scores estimated through 2014 and through 2022. A sampling unit is an alderman-by-ward-by-month cell. Cells are sampled with replacement within alderman so every named alderman remains represented in every draw while the permit history used to estimate that alderman's score changes.

Each draw repeats the complete score procedure:

1. Refit the permit-level processing-time regression.
2. Recalculate ward-month mean residuals.
3. Refit the permit-count-weighted alderman regression.
4. Recalculate the between-alderman variance, empirical-Bayes shrinkage, centering, and standardization.

The script verifies before drawing that its reconstruction of each score vintage is numerically identical to the production score.

## Propagation

For every draw, treatment is reconstructed rather than merely replacing a continuous score column:

- Density regressions receive draw-specific own-alderman scores, boundary orientation, side indicators, and side-specific distance slopes.
- Rental and sales regressions receive draw-specific stricter-side indicators.
- Permit regressions receive draw-specific changes between the 2014 origin and destination aldermen.

The eight headline models are then re-estimated. The resulting distributions isolate uncertainty from estimating the score. They are reported alongside, but are not substitutes for, the paper's clustered standard errors conditional on the production score.

`score_draw_diagnostics_summary_2000draws.csv` reports how closely each draw tracks the production score. `pair_ordering_probabilities_2000draws.csv` reports how often the production ordering is retained for each alderman comparison used by the analyses. `pair_ordering_summary_2000draws.csv` summarizes the implied observation-weighted flip rate by analysis. `headline_estimate_uncertainty_summary_2000draws.csv` compares each production estimate, its clustered standard error, and the standard deviation and percentiles generated by score uncertainty.

## Initial propagation results

The first propagation file predates the paper's current locally centered density specification. Its density results are retained only as a record of the earlier exercise and are superseded below; its rental, sales, and permit results remain current.

The median draw has a correlation of 0.90 with the through-2022 production score and 0.92 with the through-2014 production score. The expected observation-weighted probability that a production pair ordering flips is 13.1% for multifamily density, 10.8% for rents, 15.6% for sales, and 14.5% for the permit event study.

The score-bootstrap standard deviation is 60%-79% as large as the paper's clustered standard error for the four multifamily density estimates, 67% for rents, 66% for sales, and 62% for the high-discretion permit estimate. The four density estimates retain their negative sign in at least 99.65% of score draws. The rent estimate is positive in 99.95%, the sales estimate in 99.5%, and the high-discretion permit estimate is negative in every draw.

These sign frequencies condition on the outcome data and vary only the generated score. They are not p-values and do not combine score-estimation uncertainty with sampling uncertainty in the outcome regressions.

## Current density boundary specifications

`density_boundary_estimate_uncertainty_2000draws.csv` repeats the density propagation for the paper's current boundary specifications and construction-year zoning groups. In every draw, it reconstructs the binary ranking, side-specific distances, half the own-minus-neighbor score difference, and the average score of the two aldermen. Both the binary and continuous models control for that pair-average score.

The binary FAR and DUPAC estimates remain negative in 100% and 98.75% of draws. The continuous FAR and DUPAC estimates remain negative in 100% and 99.55% of draws. Score-bootstrap standard deviations are 77% as large as the clustered standard errors for both binary outcomes, and 86% and 109% as large for continuous FAR and DUPAC. These distributions supersede the earlier absolute-own-score density propagation.
